Batwara (Partition) 1947 Series

Batwara meaning separation in Hindi, is a series of poems that talks about the 1947 partition of India and Pakistan
and delicately deals with its long lasting impacts.
            बँटवारा (I)

            first,we all lived happily / together.
            190 years later,some Radcliffe sketched
            Borders on paper. Did he ever feel ashamed
            of dividing / people too?
            “No. With a pen, he painted
            blood / on religions,cleaved / roofs, and I
            was born in this distanced / world and life
            was fierce here.”



            बँटवारा ( II )

            Muslims / Hindus; swords /
            guns, deaths / babies, slashed
            in Lahore for being Hindus,
            women raped
            in Amritsar for being Muslims.

            Are you happy now? You took / our
            most beloved possession. You
            divided / us.

            “Freedom from Brits, check /
            freedom from the partition, dead
            silence.”



            बँटवारा ( III )

            “Let’s meet at Jeet’s Sweet Corner, the best since 1936”

                                                                                                                         “Only the best. I’ll be there at 5 p.m.”

                                                                                                  5:30 p.m.

      “Are you still coming?”

                                                                                                                         “I can’t find you,”

      “Are you at Jeet’s Sweet Corner,
      1936,Gawalmandi, Lahore?”

                                                                                                                         “Jeet’s Sweet Corner,
                                                                                                                          1936, Hathi Gate, Amritsar.” 1936, Hathi Gate, Amritsar.”
